Temporal Disparity Illusion

Temporal Disparity Illusion is revealing hidden flows of motion through the transformation of mathematical sequences and geometric arrangements into visual patterns. By drawing on the CIELAB color space—rooted in human visual science—color and lightness serve as precise tools to sculpt perception. In this way, mathematical algorithms are translated into perceptible motion patterns, bridging the worlds of mathematics and design.

Pumpkin Illusion is revealing how parametric variations in color, lightness, and contour geometry generate emergent motion within a static image. By embedding bicolor patterns into Kusama’s iconic pumpkin and synchronizing them with oscillating background phases, the work demonstrates a perceptual transformation: from fixed form to dynamic flow, governed by mathematical rhythm and visual computation.
